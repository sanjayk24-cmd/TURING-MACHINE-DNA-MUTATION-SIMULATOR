⟨ WHAT IS THIS? ⟩
⊞
TURING MACHINE
A theoretical model of computation by Alan Turing (1936). It reads/writes symbols on an infinite tape using a rule table to decide its next action — the basis of all modern computers.
⬡
DNA AS A TAPE
DNA is a linear sequence of nucleotides (A, T, G, C) — exactly like a Turing tape. The read/write head acts as an enzyme, scanning and mutating bases one at a time.
⇌
TRANSITIONS = MUTATIONS
Each transition rule (state, symbol) → (new state, write, move) mimics a biological mutation event: reading a codon, substituting a base, then advancing along the strand.
